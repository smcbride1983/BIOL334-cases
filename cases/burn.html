Patient Chart

John Govan

32-year-old male

History

John Govan is a 32-year-old man who sustained second-degree burns on his right forearm during a kitchen accident five days ago. The burns were initially treated at home, but over the last two days he has experienced worsening pain, swelling, and drainage from the wound.

He reports increasing fatigue, fever, and tenderness around the burn site.

Clinical Findings

  • Temperature: 101.8°F
  • Heart Rate: 110 bpm
  • WBC Count: 17,200 /µL
  • Marked neutrophilia
  • Burn wound surrounded by erythema
  • Areas of necrotic tissue present
  • Greenish-blue wound exudate
  • Distinct sweet, grape-like odor
Sample Collected: Wound Exudate Swab
