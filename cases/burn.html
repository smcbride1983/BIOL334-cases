Case 03: Post-Surgical Wound Infection

BIOL 334 Clinical Microbiology Case Study

← Back to Cases

Patient Background

Alex Ogston

45-year-old man

Alex Ogston underwent arthroscopic knee surgery two weeks ago to repair a torn meniscus. His recovery was initially uneventful, but over the last four days he has noticed increasing redness, warmth, swelling, and pain around the incision site.

The wound now drains cloudy yellow fluid, and Alex reports a low-grade fever and increasing stiffness in the affected knee. He has no signs of deep abscess formation at this time, but the surgeon is concerned that a bacterial surgical-site infection is developing.

Clinical Findings

  • Temperature: 101.4°F
  • Heart rate: 102 bpm
  • Blood pressure: 122/76 mm Hg
  • Surgical site: erythematous, warm, swollen, and tender
  • Drainage: purulent yellow fluid from incision line
  • Joint: mild stiffness with movement
  • WBC: 14,600 /µL with neutrophil predominance

Hospital Concern

The surgeon suspects a post-surgical wound infection caused by a skin-associated bacterium introduced during or shortly after the procedure. The purulent drainage and increased inflammation raise concern for a more aggressive wound pathogen rather than a low-virulence contaminant.

Sample Collected: Wound swab from purulent drainage
