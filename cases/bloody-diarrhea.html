Patient Chart

Jacob Anderson

19-year-old male

History

Jacob Anderson is a 19-year-old college student who presents to the emergency department with severe abdominal cramping and bloody diarrhea that began approximately 24 hours ago.

Three days earlier he attended a community picnic where he consumed hamburgers, potato salad, and other outdoor foods. Several friends report mild gastrointestinal illness, but Jacob's symptoms have become progressively worse.

He denies recent antibiotic use or international travel.

Clinical Findings

  • Temperature: 99.1°F
  • Heart Rate: 102 bpm
  • Blood Pressure: 110/68 mmHg
  • Severe abdominal cramping
  • Frequent bloody diarrhea
  • No leukocytes observed in stool smear
  • Mild dehydration
  • Nausea without vomiting

Initial Laboratory Results

  • WBC Count: 10,400 /µL
  • Hemoglobin: Normal
  • Platelet Count: Normal
  • Serum Creatinine: Slightly elevated
  • Urinalysis: Trace protein and red blood cells
Sample Collected: Stool Specimen
