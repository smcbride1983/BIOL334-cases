Case 6

Patient Chart

Abigail Salyers

Age: 5 days old

Setting: Neonatal Intensive Care Unit

Chief Concern: Lethargy, poor feeding, fever, apnea, and high-pitched cry

Patient Background

Abigail Salyers, a 5-day-old premature infant born at 33 weeks gestation, was admitted to the neonatal intensive care unit after delivery due to mild respiratory distress. She initially improved with oxygen therapy and feeding support.

On day 5, nurses note that she has become lethargic, is feeding poorly, and has intermittent episodes of apnea. Her temperature is elevated and she exhibits a high-pitched cry when handled.

Clinical Findings

  • Temperature: 101.5 °F
  • Heart rate: 180 bpm
  • Respiratory rate: 42 breaths/min
  • Blood pressure: 68/40 mm Hg
  • Neurologic exam: bulging fontanelle, mild neck rigidity
  • CBC: WBC 19,600/µL with neutrophil predominance
  • Blood glucose: low
Sample Collected: Cerebrospinal fluid CSF

Hospital Concern

The medical team suspects bacterial meningitis in a neonate. Premature infants have immature immune systems and are vulnerable to invasive infections acquired before birth, during delivery, or in the hospital environment.
